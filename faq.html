Exam Information

Details about the departmental examination pattern, dates and eligibility.

As per Notification-II (dated 14 May 2025), the Departmental Examination 2025 is scheduled for 8 September to 15 September 2025 (6 exam days).

📅 Application window: 23 May 2025 to 23 June 2025 · Apply online at incometaxindia.gov.in

All objective papers are online MCQ format (except ITO Paper 4 which is descriptive/offline). Each paper has 100 questions worth 100 marks with a 2-hour duration.

  • Correct answer: +1 mark
  • Wrong answer: −1/8 mark (negative marking)
  • Unattempted: 0 marks
Toppers11 replicates the official CBDT exam pattern exactly including 1/8th negative marking.

Candidates must score a minimum of 50 marks in each paper to pass. For SC/ST candidates, the minimum is 45 marks per paper.

⚠️ You must pass all 4 papers to qualify for promotion. There is no carry-forward of passed papers from previous attempts.

As per the 2025 CBDT notification, all candidates (including SC/ST) are allowed a maximum of 10 attempts for ITO and ITI examinations. There is no age bar for appearing in these exams.

Syllabus & Papers

Chapter-wise coverage and paper structure for each exam category.

Toppers11 covers all three Income Tax Departmental Promotion Exams:

  • MS (Ministerial Staff / UDC) — 4 papers
  • ITI (Income Tax Inspector) — 4 papers
  • ITO (Income Tax Officer) — 4 papers

Each exam has paper-wise mock tests and PYQs (Previous Year Questions from 2009–2024).

ITI Allied Laws (100 Marks):

  • Transfer of Property Act (~13 marks)
  • Right to Information Act, 2005 (~15 marks)
  • Civil Procedure Code (~13 marks)
  • The Indian Registration Act, 1908 (~12 marks)
  • The Indian Trust Act, 1882 (~13 marks)
  • The Indian Succession Act, 1925 (~20 marks)
  • Hindu Law (~14 marks)

ITO Allied Laws (100 Marks): Transfer of Property Act, RTI Act 2005, Civil Procedure Code, Indian Contract Act, Companies Act 2013, Indian Partnership Act 1932, IT Act 2008, Hindu Law.

Part A — Income Tax (50 Marks): Appeal, CAP, Search & Seizure, Assessment Procedures, Notices, Collection & Recovery, Refunds, Periodical Reports, CBDT Instructions, Reopening of Assessments, Time Limitations, Write-off of Taxes.

Part B — Service Rules (50 Marks): Fundamental Rules (definitions, pay, leave, joining time), Supplementary Rules (Medical Certificate, TA, Service Records), CCS (Conduct) Rules 1964, Delegation of Financial Powers Rules 1958, Pension/Gratuity, GPF Rules, CCS (R.O.P.) Rules, General Financial Rules.

  • Accounting Cycle — Journal, Ledger, Trial Balance, Financial Statements (~10 marks)
  • Partnership — Admission, Retirement, Death, Dissolution (~16 marks)
  • Joint Venture (~4 marks), Consignment (~3 marks)
  • Company Accounts (~6 marks)
  • Accounting Standards: AS-1,2,3,7,9,10,11,14,19 (~23 marks)
  • Depreciation and Hire Purchase (~10 marks)
  • Subsidiary Books, BRS, Errors & Rectification, Bills Transactions
  • Miscellaneous Theory (CSR, Analysis & Interpretation of Financials)

Yes. ITO Paper 1 carries approximately 15 marks for Case Laws. The official list (illustrative, subject to modification) includes landmark Supreme Court decisions such as:

  • Chelapalli Sugars Ltd. Vs. CIT — 98 ITR 167 (SC)
  • Mc. Dowell and Co. Ltd. Vs. Commercial Tax Officer — 154 ITR 148 (SC)
  • CIT Vs. Kelvinator of India Ltd. [2010] — 320 ITR 0561 (SC)
  • T.R.F. Ltd. Vs. CIT [2010] — 323 ITR 0397 (SC)
  • Goetze (India) Vs. CIT — 284 ITR 323 (SC)
  • And 10 more listed in the official syllabus
The case list may be modified/enlarged from year to year. Candidates are informed in advance via CBDT circulars.
